# **Article 1 - The Corporation**


## **SECTION 1 - THE ARTICLES OF SERVICE**



1. **THE PURPOSE OF THE ARTICLES OF SERVICE:** \
	a. The Articles of Service are the foundational regulations and guidelines governing the operations, behavior, and responsibilities of all Personnel within the Nova Corporation.
2. **JURISDICTION OF THE ARTICLES OF SERVICE:** \
   	a. Article 2 of the Articles of Service, also known as “Universal Regulations” applies to all Nova Corporation Personnel at a Corporation facility, within official communication servers, and in direct messages regarding official corporation duties. \
   	b. Article 3 of the Articles of Service, also known as “On-Site Regulations,” applies to all Nova Corporation Personnel actively deployed at Corporation facilities. \
    	c. The Articles of Service do not supersede any internal guidelines or documents within the Corporation. \
    	d. The Articles of Service do not apply to corporation personnel ranking Class-X or above.
3. **ENFORCING THE ARTICLES OF SERVICE:** \
    	a. Corporation personnel of the Regulations Department and Department of Operations enforce the Articles of Service. \
   	b. While enforcing the Articles of Service, the Regulations Department and Department of Operations are allocated resources as specified in Article 2, Section 2 of the Articles of Service.
4. **MAINTAINING & AMENDING THE ARTICLES OF SERVICE:** \
    	a. The Legislative Branch of the Regulations Department has been tasked with maintaining the Articles of Service. \
    	b. To amend the Articles of Service a proposal must be sent to, and passed by, the Legislative Branch and Board of Advisors.


## **SECTION 2 - THE STRUCTURE OF THE CORPORATION**

1. **CLEARANCE CLASSES:** \
    a. Class-X**: **The highest clearance class granted to corporation personnel ranking Class-X. They possess full access to all Corporation property and the authority to regulate entities classified at the White threat level. \
    b. Class-O: The second-highest clearance class, awarded to members who have demonstrated exceptional loyalty and importance to the Corporation. This class grants near-complete access to Corporation property.  \
    c. Class-A: Assigned to administrative Department members, granting them increased access to Corporation property and the responsibility of evaluating Class-D applications. To receive a Class-A clearance, a corporation member must meet the requirements of the High Member Security Act. \
    d. Class-B: Designated for Personnel with substantial experience within a Department, providing them with greater access to entities. These Personnel are typically in a medium rank position within their Department. \
    e. Class-C: Assigned to Personnel who have gained the third rank in a Department. \
    f. Class-D: The entry-level clearance class for Nova Corporation Personnel. This class is application-based and grants limited access to Corporation facilities and entities. \
    g. Class-E: Assigned to Nova Corporation Personnel who have been suspended for violating the Articles of Service. \
        i. Regulations regarding Class - E sentences are defined by the Class - E Act and **[Class-E Board](https://github.com/Meta-Method/Nova-Corporation-Laws/blob/main/Essential/Class%20-%20E%20Board.md)**.
2. **VIP PERSONNEL:** \
    a. Personnel holding the rank of Class-A and above are classified as VIP Personnel and should be treated as high-security individuals. \
    b. Foreign visitors from groups that actively maintain relations with the Nova Corporation are also considered VIP Personnel. \
    c. As high-security individuals, VIP Personnel are prioritized during evacuations and are entitled to protection from the Task Force Detachment "The Armed Gentlemen."
3. **BOARD OF ADVISORS:** \
    a. The Board of Advisors is a group of Departmental Representatives responsible for proposing, discussing, and authorizing laws and policies for the Nova Corporation.  \
    b. The Board of Advisors is defined and regulated by the regulations outlined in the **[Board of Advisors Act](https://github.com/Meta-Method/Nova-Corporation-Laws/blob/main/Essential/Board%20of%20Advisors.md)**.
4. **INNOVATION DEPARTMENT:** \
    a. The Innovation Department is responsible for all research in the Corporation, including, but not limited to, conducting expeditions, and finding new ways to utilize entities. \
    b. Corporation personnel of the Nova Corporation actively assigned to the Innovation Department on-site are officially designated as “Innovation Personnel.” \
    c. Innovation Personnel holding the ranks of "Expert Innovator" and "Head Innovator" are classified as part of the Innovation Department’s High Command. \
    d. Innovation Personnel holding the ranks of "Deputy Director" and "Director" are classified as part of the Innovation Department’s Directorate.
5. **SECURITY CORPS:** \
    a. The Security Corps is the combative force of the Corporation, divided into 5 different groups.  \
        i. “Main Force” are general members of the Security Corps.  \
        ii. “Iron Fist” is responsible for large-scale combative actions. \
        iii. “The Armed Gentlemen” are responsible for protecting VIP Personnel. \
        iv. “The Crazies” are responsible for the containment of entities. \
        v. “Nothing To See Here” are general special operations. \
    b. Corporation personnel of the Nova Corporation actively assigned to the Security Corps are officially designated as “Security Personnel.” \
    c. Security Personnel holding the ranks of "Security Supervisor", “Captain”, and "Security Major" are classified as part of the Security Corps’ High Command. \
    d. Security Personnel holding the ranks of "Commander" and "Head Commander" are classified as part of the Security Corps’ Directorate.
6. **ENGINEERING DEPARTMENT:** \
    a. The Engineering Department is responsible for the logistics of the Corporation, including, but not limited to, maintaining vehicles, and manufacturing and stocking ores in various sites. \
    b. Corporation Personnel of the Nova Corporation actively assigned to the Engineering Department are officially designated as “Engineering Personnel.” \
    c. Engineering Personnel holding the ranks of "Foreperson" and "Coordinator" are classified as part of the Engineering Department’s High Command. \
    d. Engineering Personnel holding the ranks of "Associate Director" and "Director of Engineering" are classified as part of the Engineering Department’s Directorate.
7. **REGULATIONS DEPARTMENT:** \
    a. The Regulations Department is responsible for the maintenance and amending of the Corporation’s overall legislation, alongside investigating infractions and managing community relations and external affairs. \
        i. The Legislative Branch is responsible for maintaining the Articles of Service and its subsidiary legislation. \
        ii. The Investigative Branch is responsible for investigating infractions of Corporation legislation. \
        iii. The Liaison Ancillary is responsible for external affairs and internal community relations. \
    b. Corporation Personnel of the Nova Corporation actively assigned to the Regulations Department are officially designated as “Regulations Personnel.” \
    c. Regulations Personnel holding the ranks of "Chief Regulating Official", “Division Director”, and "Division Overseer" are classified as part of the Regulations Department’s High Command. \
    d. Regulations Personnel holding the ranks of "Assistant Director" and "Director" are classified as part of the Regulations Department’s Directorate.
8. **DEPARTMENT OF OPERATIONS:** \
    a. The Department of Operations is responsible for the internal security of the Corporation, including, but not limited to, investigating corruption, handling information breaches, and much more. \
    b. Corporation Personnel of the Nova Corporation actively assigned to the Department of Operations are officially designated as “Operations Personnel.” \
    c. Operations Personnel holding the ranks of "Drone" are classified as part of the Department of Operations’ High Command. \
    d. Operations Personnel holding the ranks of “Executive Drone” and “Cardinal Drone” are classified as part of the Department of Operations Directorate.

# **Article 2 - Universal Regulations** \

## **SECTION 1 - CODE OF CONDUCT** \

1. **RESPECT & PROFESSIONALISM:** \
    a. All Corporation Personnel are to treat other Corporation Personnel with respect, without discrimination or exception. \
    b. Corporation Personnel are to act maturely and professionally while performing duties on-site. \
    c. Needless remarks in a hurtful and offensive tone are strictly prohibited. \
    d. Slander of departments in any form is prohibited.
2. **IMPERSONATION:** \
    a. All Corporation Personnel are prohibited from impersonating any Personnel or hostile forces, both within any Nova Corporation Site and on any Nova Corporation communications platforms. This includes:
   	i. Falsely claiming to be a member of a Department or sub-Department. 
    	ii. Acting on behalf of a Department, sub-department, or individual without explicit permission from the respective authority.
3. **FALSE INFORMATION:** \
    a. Members of the Nova Corporation are forbidden from intentionally spreading false information to anyone except in certain circumstances.
    b. Security and Operations Personnel may spread misinformation to gain a tactical advantage against hostile forces.
4. **CORRUPTION & CONSPIRACY:** \
    a. Creating or joining groups that oppose or raid any official or affiliated Nova Corporation group, engaging in bribery (to dishonestly persuade someone to act in one’s favor with anything of value), and conducting dishonest actions or abusing power for personal gain is strictly prohibited.
    b. All Corporation Personnel are forbidden from encouraging others to violate the Articles of Service. \
5. **INSUBORDINATION:** \
    a. Corporation personnel are to follow orders from those with authority, as defined by Article 3; Section 5.
    b. An order should not be followed if it breaks the Articles of Service unless it is issued by a Corporation Personnel with Class-X clearance.
6. **ALTERNATIVE ACCOUNTS:** \
    a. The utilization of alternative accounts is prohibited within the Nova Corporation.
    b. For account transfers, personnel must contact the Department of Operations (DoO) through their [communications server.](https://discord.gg/PXrBpSUSxy)
7. **IMPEDING DUTIES:** \
    a. All Corporation Personnel are not allowed to attempt or succeed in directly or indirectly impeding another Corporation personnel’s duties.
8. **INFORMATION RESTRICTIONS:** \
    a. All Nova Corporation documents are accessible to Personnel unless specified otherwise:
        i. A document is considered classified if it can only be accessed by joining a department/division.
        ii. Personnel with Class-X clearance can authorize access to restricted documents.
        iii.  Corporation personnel ranking Class-X or above may temporarily restrict any document for any reason.
    b. Should a breach of restricted information or material occur, the [Information Breach Procedures](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Rt7kJIjdAkJBPJd7X9lhgHkTiVqTZGGTkTMoNf1Wzu8/edit) must be followed. 
9. **COOPERATION WITH HOSTILE FORCES:** \
    a. Corporation Personnel are strictly prohibited from cooperating with hostile forces in any form.
        i. This prohibition includes sharing restricted information, divulging department plans, or providing any information that could benefit hostile forces or that they do not have access to. It also             includes supporting them in planning activities, attacks, or any other form of sabotage against the Corporation.
    b. Corporation Personnel may not utilize any vendors within the Test Subject Zone.

## **SECTION 2 - INFRACTION REGULATIONS**

1. **OBLIGATION TO COMPLY:**
    a. Any Clause within these Articles is to be followed at all times by any Personnel bound to these Articles of Service, from the time of admittance to the Corporation to the time of departure to the         Corporation, unless authorized to violate a Clause by a corporation personnel ranking Class-X or above.
    b. All Corporation Personnel receiving a formal inquiry regarding any case must respond accurately with their side in the situation, provide all possible available evidence, and cooperate with the           below-specified Personnel.
        i. Only Regulations Personnel may conduct formal inquiries with probable cause regarding the Articles of Service.
        ii. Operations Personnel may conduct formal inquiries under any circumstances.
2. **CONFESSIONS:**
    a. Should a Corporation Personnel state that they violated or will violate the Articles of Service, they may be treated as a suspect of any subsequent violation.
    b. Any evidence of said statements will be added as evidence during the investigations.
    c. Lying about violations of the Articles of Service is to be considered a violation of this Clause and Article 2; Section 1; Clause 3.
3. **OBSTRUCTION OF JUSTICE:**
    a. Should any Corporation Personnel view a perceived violation of the Article of Service, they must report it to the Regulations Department or Department of Operations Public Server.
    b. Corporation Personnel may not attempt or stop Personnel from reporting an infraction.
    c. Personnel may not abstain from questioning.
4. **FALSE REPORTS:**
    a. All Personnel are forbidden from fabricating false reports on Corporation Personnel stating they violated any regulations to a Departmental high command member or the Regulations Department and
       Department of Operations.
6. **EVASION OF PUNISHMENT:**
    a. Under no circumstance should a member of the Corporation attempt to bypass or evade punishment, investigation, or introduction of punishment, including, but not limited to, leaving while detained         without permission, leaving and rejoining the group while Class-E'd, or anything similar.
**6) RIGHT TO REQUEST PUNISHMENT INFORMATION:**
    a.  All Corporation Personnel have the right to send a formal request for information regarding their punishments (e.g., Strikes, Class-E sentences, Discord bans) to the Regulations Department and Discord Moderation.
        i. Corporation Personnel also have the right to formally request information about their internal Department punishments from their High Command.
    b. All formal requests for information must be made to aid them in the appeals process for each specific Department/sub-component.
    c. Formal requests may be denied for cases where their punishment is unappealable. 
    d. For appeals regarding Class-E, the appellee must be shown where to find details about their punishment.
    e. Requests may be denied if witnesses in the evidence request to be anonymous.
**7) INSTIGATING OTHERS:**
    a. All Corporation Personnel are forbidden from encouraging others to break the clauses within the Articles of Service and the Department Regulations Codex, including but not limited to: persuade other personnel from testifying against violations from the Article of Service etc.

## **SECTION 3 - LEGISLATIVE IMMUNITIES**

1. **OVERRIDING CLAUSES:**
    a. Operations Personnel may temporarily override any Clause under Article 3 Section 1 and Section 4. with proper reasoning through public declaration unless such an action will put overall Nova              Corporation Operations in jeopardy or directly or indirectly cause harm to a Corporation Personnel ranking Class-X or above.
    b. The Armed Gentlemen may temporarily override Article 3, with proper reasoning, to perform their duties, including but not limited to hostage situations and VIP protections.
1. **OVERRIDING DEPARTMENTAL COMMANDS:**
    c. Operations Personnel may not directly override Departmental commands from said Department's or Sub-Department’s High Command unless the situation is in a critical state and an improper decision           could result in a major loss of Nova Corporation Personnel, property, and information.
2. **DEPARTMENTAL GUIDELINES EXEMPTIONS:**
    a. Departmental Personnel are exempt from any clause that interferes with their duties listed in their internal guidelines.
3. **TERMINATION BY OPERATIONS PERSONNEL:**
    a. Operations Personnel may terminate any Corporation Personnel below the rank of Class-X, should their lives be purposefully put in danger by the stated Corporation Personnel.

# **Article 3 - On-Site Regulations**

## **SECTION 1 - GENERAL REGULATIONS**

1. **DRESS CODE:**
    a. All Corporation Personnel below Class-O must follow proper attire rules. 
    b. Outfits worn must not be immature, inappropriate, suggestive, or subversive in any form. 
    c. Outfits must not obscure the rank title or name tag to the point where it is no longer reasonably visible. 
    d. Corporation Personnel may not wear uniforms associated with or similar to those of another division within the Corporation or any hostile group unless authorized by Security Personnel ranking Security Major or above. The exceptions to this sub-clause are members of the Department of Operations and the Task Force Detachment “Nothing To See Here.”
    e. Outfits worn must not have Roblox accessories that are unprofessional, immature, or as large nor larger than the player avatar.
2. **ENDANGERMENT:**
    a. Corporation Personnel shall not directly or indirectly threaten the lives of other Corporation Personnel.
    b. Corporation Personnel may terminate escaping Test Subjects, and other Corporation Personnel breaking this Clause if there are no Security Personnel on-site. 
    c. All non-combative Corporation Personnel except Operations Personnel may never actively seek danger or interfere with Security Personnel gun fights and must always seek to reside in a safe location        within a corporation-owned site. 
        i. If a Corporation member is unable to attain a safe location in time to avoid hostiles, said Corporation member may defend themselves without breaking any other Clause in the Article of Service.
        ii. In the scenario of hostiles in the lower Entity Area during an official Innovation Department test described under Article 3; Section 3, all participating in the test are to follow the                    procedure as written in their respective guidelines.
    d. Engineering Personnel are granted and permitted to craft and utilize the AK-47 for defending Engineering Department work zones.
        ii. Engineering Department "Work Zones" are defined as any locations with Engineering Department activities such as depots, mining, ore production, mining operations, crafting, etc.
3. **CORPORATION PERSONNEL TERMINATION CRITERIA:**
    a. Corporation Personnel may be terminated by Security Personnel if one or more of the following conditions is satisfied:
        i. They are trespassing.
        ii. They are directly interacting with entities without approval.
        iii. They are in violation of the firearms regulations.
        iv. They are impeding duties.
        v. They are posing a threat to Corporation Personnel or property
        vi. They are designated “Kill on Sight” by a Class-O or above.
        vii. They are interfering with the safety of a VIP.
        viii. The person(s) may not be terminated under any circumstance if they are at or above the position of Class-O, or are considered Operations Personnel.
4. **TEST SUBJECT TERMINATION CRITERIA:**
    a. Test Subjects may be terminated by the Security Personnel at any time if one or more of the following conditions is satisfied:
        i. Said Test Subject wields any object besides a handsaw, pickaxe, jacket, fabric, or laundry.
        ii. Said Test Subject surpassed the allocated three warnings.
        iii. Said Test Subject becomes physically unresponsive to commands or inquiries for several minutes.
        iv. Said Test Subject attempts to escape Nova Corporation custody and/or cause disruptions to the running of the area.
        v. Said Test Subject intentionally or unintentionally breaches an entity, unless granted permission by an Innovation Personnel overseeing a test, and a Task Force Detachment “The Crazies” operative.
        vi. Said Test Subject is under an anomalous effect unless it is for testing purposes.
        vii. A termination order is given by the Innovation Personnel presiding over the test, OR the said Test Subject is permanently affected by any anomalous effect after testing has concluded.
        viii. Said Test Subject intentionally passes the white line in front of the Lower Test Subject Zone airlock gate or comes into physical contact with the airlock windows.
        ix. Said Test Subject is impeding Security Personnel during a combat situation.
        x. Said Test Subject is under reasonable suspicion of wielding a dangerous object and is in a position to danger a VIP.
        xi. Said Test Subject is wearing items or clothing that do not come with the default test subject morph, such as but not limited to: gas masks, departmental or hostile uniforms, ballistic helmets, or a bulletproof vest.
5. **HOSTAGE REGULATIONS:**
    a. Personnel ranking Class-A must not redeploy, leave, reset, or otherwise during a hostage situation unless there is a valid reason.
        i. If delimbed or detained through fences or walls, this rule does not apply. 
    b. Personnel ranking Class-A and above may comply with hostage takers' demands, including the violation of the Articles of Service given that they can find no other way of self-preservation except transferring valuable information regarding on-site function, or classified information and/or documentation from internal sources.
6. **REGULATIONS CHECKPOINTS:**
    a. All Personnel are to heed checkpoints manned by Regulations Personnel unless there is an emergency that requires immediate attention or said Personnel are fleeing from danger.
        i. Members of the Task Force Detachment "The Crazies" are not required to heed manned Entity Area checkpoints.
        ii. Leaving a checkpoint after being greeted by Regulations Personnel before being given permission to is considered a violation of this clause.
        iii. All members of the Security Corps and Engineering Department are not required to heed or stop at manned checkpoints during a Red Wolves raid.
4. **EVACUATIONS:**
    a. Site-wide evacuations are when evacuations cover an individual site.
        i. Evacuations due to a riot are led by Engineering and Regulations Personnel and have Personnel be evacuated to the Entity Area, or any other safe site if unavailable.
        ii. Evacuations due to a large number of hostile forces, or if the site has been captured, are led by Engineering and Regulations Personnel and have Personnel be evacuated to the nearest safe site.
        iii. Evacuations due to a new entity or breach are led by The Crazies and have Personnel evacuated to the nearest safe site.
    b. Area-wide evacuations are when evacuations cover the entire area and are evacuated to an individual site, off-site, or the Nuclear Shelter depending on relevant high-ranking personnel orders.
        iv. Evacuations due to a Nova Dominion raid are led by Security Personnel.
        v. Evacuations due to a new entity or breach are led by The Crazies, Regulations, and Engineering Department.
        vi. Evacuations due to a nuclear strike are led by Iron Fist.

14. **OTHER AREAS:**
    a. Any area not listed here will have temporary regulatory control by Department commands for their specific Departments.

## **SECTION 3 - TEST REGULATIONS**

1. **TEST VALIDITY:**
    a. All testing must be logical and properly enacted, and not cost the Nova Corporation an unnecessary amount of resources.
        i. All testing must be relatively safe, and not needlessly cost the lives of Corporation Personnel on purpose.
        ii. All Test Subjects and Security Personnel participating are to be informed of what they are to do before the test occurs.
        iii. All Test Subjects and Security Personnel are not to be subjected to extra steps not related to the test specifically.
        iv. All tests must seem hypothetically possible in the sense that the testing question does not seem random. An invalid question is if one action causes or is related to a completely different reaction.
        v. Any test conducted to terminate an anomaly requires majority approval from a Class-X overwatch vote and one member from the ID Directorate.
2. **TEST SUBJECT RETRIEVAL & RETURN**
    a. All Personnel present within a test must return to an area they have access to, as specified under Article 3, Section 2.
        i. Test Subjects, remaining after the test is concluded, may not be terminated by Security Personnel and must be escorted back to the Test Subject Zone unless Articles 3 Section 2 Clause 3 is satisfied.
        ii. Test Subjects, remaining after the test is concluded, may not be tested on again, unless there is the Personnel necessary to conduct the test and both the test host and Security Personnel present agree upon it. 
3. **TEST QUESTIONING & SPECTATING**
    a. Corporation Personnel ranking Class-O and above, Regulations Personnel, and Operations Personnel may question any Innovation Personnel regarding any aspect of their test.
    b. Said Innovation Personnel must answer truthfully to the best of their ability.
        i. Said Innovation Personnel must allow the listed Personnel above to spectate the entirety of the test as applicable.
        ii. Said Innovation Personnel may disallow the listed Personnel above to question them and spectate if the question test has been restricted by the Innovation Department’s assigned Personnel ranking Class-X, and proof must be provided at the time of request.
4. **BREACHED ENTITIES:**
    a. Should an entity be spotted at any facility not currently under siege, Task Force Detachment “The Crazies” gain full control of the facility's Personnel, utilities, and vehicles. 
        i. Entity is defined as an anomalous person, place, or thing that typically does not follow the current knowledge of reality. The Nova Corporation captures and contains entities for Innovation and Research.
        ii. Beings afflicted by an entity such as 007, 020 armor, and 020 orbs do not count as an entity breach, beings afflicted by 008, 013, and 012 do however count. Beings in possession of an entity such as 014 and 010 also count as an entity breach.
    b. All corporation personnel are required to report all and any entity breaches to a member of The Crazies, at the moment the breach is spotted.

## **SECTION 4 - PROPERTY MANAGEMENT**

1. **UTILIZATION OF CORPORATION RESOURCES & PROPERTY:**
    a. Property of the Corporation is to be utilized to its fullest, including but not limited to, test subjects, entities, and crafted utilities. Nothing may be wasted or overutilized intentionally or egregiously.
    b. The Engineering Department holds full jurisdiction over all ores in the Nova Corporation, except for property claimed by the Department of Operations, held in the Entity Area or the Forward Operating Base which falls under the control of the Iron Fist. In these situations, permission is required from the relevant Personnel.
        i. In the event of an emergency where no Engineering Personnel are available, Security Personnel may utilize resources to protect the interests of the Corporation.
    c. Task Force Detachment "Iron Fist" has authority over all vehicles within the Nova Corporation. If they are unavailable, authority over unarmoured Vehicles falls to the Engineering Personnel.
    d. The property should be immediately disposed of when it can no longer serve any useful purpose.
2. **KEYCARDS:**
    a. Corporation Personnel may not pick up keycards of higher clearances than they have by default unless they are disposing of them or transporting them to a secure location.
        i. All keycards are to be handled securely, leaving keycards in crates or unprotected for extended periods knowingly is prohibited.
        ii. Exception: In situations of danger, security may pick up keycards to prevent unauthorized acquisition, including those that may drop. This exception lasts until the situation is resolved, after which the keycards must either be disposed of or transferred to a valid holder.
3. **DETAIN:**
    a. All Corporation Personnel who have a detain by default may utilize it according to their internal guidelines unless they are using it for a tactical advantage while in combat against hostiles.
    b. Regulations personnel internally ranking Chief Regulating Official or above on the Regulations Department team are granted permission to issue a Detain-on-Sight order for a Nova Corporation Member if deemed necessary and with valid reason.
4. **RADIO MANIPULATION:**
    a. Misuse of the radio is strictly prohibited, which includes, but is not limited to, providing misinformation to Corporation Personnel, clogging up the radio, or anything similar.
    b. Only Engineering, Security, and Operations Personnel may manipulate and manage objects that interfere with, or extend radio signals.
5. **EMP PLACEMENT:**
    a.  EMP jammers may only be placed by Security or Operations Personnel, and may not majorly obstruct any operations.
6. **CONTROL ROOM:**
    a. Security, Innovation, Regulations, and Corporation Personnel ranking Class-A+ may access the Entity Area control room.
        i. The lights may be disabled if it is relevant to containing an entity, if it is relevant to a test if there are more than 3 hostiles, if there is an active raid or site siege, or if there are any Super-Soldier’ed hostiles within the Entity Area.
